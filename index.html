The Jewell Assessment

Your AI strategy has a blind spot.

Every AI readiness checklist measures the same things. Data quality. Infrastructure. Talent. None of it explains why organizations with excellent data still fail while organizations with mediocre data figure it out. The difference isn't readiness — it's taste. The strategic judgment to know what to deploy, when to kill it, and what happens after the AI works.

For executives evaluating AI strategy. For technical leads navigating implementation. For anyone trying to understand where their organization actually stands.

4% of organizations achieve scaled AI deployment. The other 96% don't fail on technology.

What We Keep Seeing

Foundation

$200K AI pilot. Dead at month three. The cause: 40% of their claims data was locked in scanned PDFs nobody knew about. The AI worked. The data wasn't there.

Architecture

92% accurate demand forecasting model. Useless. The procurement workflow it fed required three levels of human approval. By the time anyone acted, the market had moved.

Accountability

40-page AI governance policy. Board-approved. Nobody's read it. When audited: 60% of data pipelines had zero lineage tracking. The policy couldn't describe what it was supposed to govern.

These aren't edge cases. This is the pattern I keep seeing.

02

Framework

Five layers. Each one builds on the one beneath it. Fix your foundation and you'll hit your architecture wall. Fix that and accountability becomes the constraint. It's always the layer you're not looking at.

5 Taste Do the people making AI decisions have the judgment to get it right? 4 Culture Can someone in your organization say 'this AI initiative should stop' and actually be heard? 3 Accountability When the AI breaks at 2am on a Saturday, who specifically gets called? 2 Architecture Are your workflows worth automating, or are you speeding up the mess? 1 Foundation Is your data actually accessible — or does it just exist somewhere?
